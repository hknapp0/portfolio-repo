USDA Homepage Redesign

When challenged with the task of redesigning a government agency website, my teammates and I saw a ton of potential in the USDA.gov website. We immediately saw that the homepage was cluttered with unnecessary information, and got to work right away improving it!

Our team: Haley Knapp, Andi Ives, Kaylin Price

Our Role: UX/UI Designer

Tools: Miro, Figma, InVision, Google Drive, Trello, Canva, Slack, Zoom

View Clickable Desktop Prototype View Clickable Mobile Prototype

The Problem

The USDA website allows users to access USDA information no matter where they are whether itâ€™s an office, grocery store, or the middle of a farmer's field, and regardless of what device or browser they're using. The problem is the information that users are looking for is difficult to find.

The Research

After defining the problem, my teammates and I conducted usability tests on the original USDA.gov homepage. We grouped our findings into an importance matrix and empathy map. With that information we were able to get a firm grasp of what we needed to change for a better user experience.


View Miro Board

We annotated the original USDA.gov homepage, which allowed us to shape our new site map. The main focus was changing the information hierarchy in the navigation and homepage content.

We created lo-fi wireframes to build a base, then after designing a style guide, built our final wireframes. Our main focus was redesigning the information hierarchy. Making sure everything was clear and concise to our users, we continued with our final wireframes making it a clickable prototype.

The Solution

Redesigning the information hierarchy and simplifying the the content made it easier for users to navigate and asbord the content they were looking for. This also allowed users to find what they were looking for with less frustration.
