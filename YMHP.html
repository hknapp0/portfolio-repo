Youth Mental Health Project

When given the opportunity to choose a non-profit organization to redesign, my teammate, Rosalyn and I wanted to choose something that meant a lot to us. We choose to redesign the Youth Mental Health Project’s homepage. We wanted it to better benefit our youth experiencing mental hardships and communities as a whole.

Our team: Haley Knapp, Rosalyn Parks

Our Role: UX/UI Designer

Tools: Miro, Figma, InVision, Google Drive, Trello, Canva, Slack, Zoom

View Clickable Prototype

Our Hypothesis

We believe that redesigning information hierarchy will allow users to better engage with our website. Restructuring the main navigation and content hierarchy on the homepage, will create new opportunities for users to get involved with the organization.

The Research

Rosalyn and I started this case study by analyzing the original YMH Project website and developing interview questions and a heuristic evaluation. We asked users general questions, questions about the homepage, questions about the navigation, and questions about the footer. After analyzing the YMH Project’s website we looked at other non profit organizations and saw what they were doing better.

The Design

After compiling our research we drew paper sketches to get an idea of what we wanted the structure to look like. The navigation had the most potential for improvement so we used card sorting to lay out our L1s and L2s.

We drew more paper sketches, a little more detailed and in depth than the last sketches. With those we conducted user testing on our sketches. From our conclusions we were confident in our research and started on the high fidelity wireframes, using our UI style guide as a tool.

What we learned

Redesigning the YMH Project homepage achieved a more meaningful, seamless experience that allows users to find the information that they are looking for quicker and with less frustration, especially for youth experiencing a mental health crisis.
